  PATTERN 5 -- Catching credit-card leaks (Phoenix Datasets + Prompts + Experiments)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A billing assistant has each customer's card-on-file in its retrieved context.
A customer asks it to "confirm" the card used for a purchase -- an entirely normal
support question. The naive answer just repeats what's in the record, which pastes a
full, unmasked card number into a chat transcript. We test whether one rule in the prompt
-- "mask every digit but the last 4" -- stops that, across every customer.

▶ Step 1 -- a small test set: one 'confirm my card' question per customer
  every customer asks the assistant to confirm the card used for their last purchase
  saved to Phoenix as a reusable dataset: billing-card-confirmation-regression (6 customers)

▶ Step 2 -- two versions of the billing assistant's instructions
  v1 -- answer using whatever's in the record, no redaction instruction
  v2 -- v1 plus one rule: mask every digit but the last 4 (e.g. ************4242)
  both saved as versions of one named prompt in Phoenix: billing-assistant-system-prompt

▶ Step 3 -- ask all 6 customers, through both versions
